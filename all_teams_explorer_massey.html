What Is Massey Rating?

Massey is a linear system solved over all teams in a pool at once. Let each team have unknown rating r_i. For each match, define observed margin m = (points_for - points_against) from Team A's perspective.

Build matrix M and vector p so the model is M r = p:

1) Off-diagonal terms: for teams i != j, M_ij = -n_ij, where n_ij is the number of times teams i and j played.

2) Diagonal terms: M_ii = sum(n_ij) across all opponents of team i (total games for team i).

3) Right-hand side: p_i = total signed point margin for team i across all its games.

Interpretation: each equation is the net margin balance, approximated by rating differences over that schedule. The least-squares/linear solve finds ratings whose pairwise differences best explain observed margins across the whole network.

The system is singular up to an additive constant (adding the same value to every rating does not change differences), so one normalization constraint is applied (for example, fixing one equation/anchor) to obtain a unique solution.

Practical meaning: rating gaps are the key output. If r_i - r_j is positive, team i is estimated stronger than team j; larger gaps imply stronger expected advantage.

Model in use: massey. Ratings update whenever new match files are imported and the pipeline is rerun.
